Windows

Method 1:

  1. Download Shadowsocks for Windows.
  2. Extract the archive, and double-click on the Shadowsocks.exe file.
  3. Make sure the QR code below is centered and completely visible, right-click on the Shadowsocks system tray icon, and go to Servers > Scan QRCode from Screen...

Method 2:

  1. You may also go to Servers > New server and manually complete the following steps:
    • Enter 178.62.33.140 as the Server IP.
    • Enter 8388 as the Server Port.
    • Enter OpTBYD82WQzm29TZPgfJqA== as the Password.
    • The Local Port should be 1080 and the Encryption Method should be aes-256-cfb.
    • Click Save.
  2. Click on the Shadowsocks menu tray icon and select Enable.
  3. That's it! You can verify that your traffic is being routed properly by looking up your IP address on Google. It should say Your public IP address is 178.62.33.140.

OS X

Method 1:

  1. Download ShadowsocksX.
  2. Double-click the DMG, and drag the icon into your Applications folder.
  3. Launch ShadowsocksX. You will be prompted to enter your password so that your system proxy settings can be modified.
  4. Look for the Shadowsocks paper plane icon in your menu bar and click on it.
  5. Make sure the QR code below is centered and completely visible, and choose Scan QR Code from Screen...

Method 2:

  1. You may also configure the connection by going to Servers, selecting Open Server Preferences..., and clicking the + button on the sidebar:
    1. Enter 178.62.33.140 and 8388 in the Address fields.
    2. Make sure aes-256-cfb is selected for the Encryption value.
    3. Enter OpTBYD82WQzm29TZPgfJqA== as the Password.
    4. Click OK.
  2. Click on the Shadowsocks icon in the menu bar again, and choose Global Mode.
  3. You can use the Shadowsocks icon to enable/disable the VPN. The color of the icon will change based on whether or not it is active.
  4. That's it! You can verify that your traffic is being routed properly by looking up your IP address on Google. It should say Your public IP address is 178.62.33.140.

Linux

  1. Download the latest Shadowsocks linux clients for different Linux distributions.
  2. From the command line, run ss-qt5

  3. Click Connection, select Add, and you can:

    • Make sure the QR code below is centered and completely visible, click on Scan QR Code on Screnn

    • Or Choose Manually, then fill in the configuration information as follows:

      • Server: 178.62.33.140
      • Port: 8388
      • Password: OpTBYD82WQzm29TZPgfJqA==
      • Encryption Method: aes-256-cfb

  4. Once you have Shadowsocks running locally, you'll need to forward your browser traffic through the SOCKS proxy it provides.

Configuring Firefox to connect through a SOCKS proxy

  1. Click the Menu button next to the Home icon to the right of the address bar.
  2. Click Options.
  3. Click the Advanced icon.
  4. Go to the Network tab.
  5. Click the Settings button to Configure how Firefox connects to the Internet.
  6. Choose Manual proxy configuration.
  7. Enter 127.0.0.1 and Port 1080 on the SOCKS Host line.
  8. Select Remote DNS. This configures Firefox to send all DNS requests through the SOCKS proxy. This will protect you against DNS poisoning and ensure that false DNS entries cannot be used to censor your access.
  9. Click OK.
  10. Click OK again to close the Firefox preferences window.
  11. You should be good to go! You can verify that your traffic is being routed properly by looking up your IP address on Google. It should say Your public IP address is 178.62.33.140.

Android

  1. Download Shadowsocks for Android from Google Play. If Google Play is blocked in your country, you can download a latest release apk file from Github.
  2. Launch the application.
  3. Tap the Shadowsocks icon in the top-left of the screen.
  4. Tap the + icon in the bottom-right.
  5. Select Scan QR code.
  6. Use your phone to scan this image, or if you are using your phone right now you can choose the Manual Settings option and copy and paste the information from the Linux section above:

  1. Tap the plane icon on the top.
  2. Accept the Android VPN connection warning.
  3. You can verify that your traffic is being routed properly by looking up your IP address on Google. It should say Your public IP address is 178.62.33.140.

iOS

Unfortunately, support for Shadowsocks on iOS is terminated, and the app on AppStore is out-of-date. One alternative is:

  1. Download a paid app Potatso and launch it.
  2. You will be prompted to sign into the iTunes Store the first time you run Potatso.
  3. Tap the More icon in the lower-right of the screen.
  4. Tap Import From QRCode.
  5. Use your phone to scan this image, or if you are using your phone right now you can manually add a new proxy from the Home tab and copy and paste the information from the Linux section above:

  1. Enter a name when the Add a new proxy prompt appears, and tap OK. You'll see a confirmation that the import was successful.
  2. Tap the Home icon.
  3. Tap Proxy and select the name of the proxy that you just imported.
  4. Tap the blue Connect button.
    • If this is your first time running Potatso, iOS will ask you to verify that the application should have permission to add VPN configurations. Tap Allow and follow the instructions.
  5. You can verify that your traffic is being routed properly by looking up your IP address on Google. It should say Your public IP address is 178.62.33.140.
